UI/UX Case Study

KFC Redesign

A UX redesign project focused on improving the KFC checkout experience for takeout orders by making the payment flow more flexible, convenient, and user-friendly.

View Case Study Explore More Projects

Overview

This project was created as a UX audit and redesign of the KFC takeout checkout experience. The goal was to identify a key usability issue in the payment process and redesign the flow to better support user convenience, faster decision-making, and a smoother ordering experience.

Challenge

The main problem identified in the existing checkout experience was the lack of payment flexibility. Users were limited to paying only by credit card, which created friction for customers who preferred alternatives such as Apple Pay or paying at the store. This reduced convenience and made the checkout process feel less accommodating.

Solution

I redesigned the payment step to include additional payment options that better matched user expectations and real-world ordering habits. The updated concept introduced more flexibility into the checkout flow while keeping the interface simple, clear, and aligned with a fast-food mobile ordering experience.

Design Focus

The redesign focused on usability, accessibility, and reducing friction in the payment journey. The concept aimed to create a smoother path from cart to confirmation by improving clarity, choice, and overall convenience within the takeout ordering process.

Tools

The project combined UX analysis and interface design methods to create a more effective and user-centered ordering experience.
