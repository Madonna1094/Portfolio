Overview

Wild Rose Foundry is an e-commerce style marketplace website created to highlight handcrafted products from Alberta-based makers. The platform brings together multiple vendors, organized product categories, featured product sections, and detailed product pages into one cohesive digital storefront. The project focuses on creating a warm, curated shopping experience while helping users browse products, learn about each maker, and add items to their cart with ease.

Challenge

The main challenge was to design a marketplace experience that could handle a growing inventory and multiple independent vendors without feeling cluttered. The website needed to balance product discovery, brand storytelling, and usability. It also had to make navigation simple across categories, vendors, product listings, and individual product pages while maintaining a handcrafted and premium visual feel.

Solution

I created a structured multi-page website that organizes the shopping journey clearly from the homepage. Users can browse featured products, view all products, explore vendor profiles, open individual product pages, and use add-to-cart actions throughout the site. The content hierarchy and layout were designed to make the platform feel approachable, organized, and visually consistent across different sections.

Key Features

  • Homepage hero section introducing the brand and marketplace concept
  • Featured product area to highlight selected handcrafted items
  • Category and vendor navigation for faster product discovery
  • All products page with a larger catalog and pagination support
  • Dedicated vendor pages with booth information and brand storytelling
  • Product detail pages with pricing, categories, variants, and vendor details
  • Cart functionality supported by add-to-cart actions across the site
  • Consistent layout that keeps the experience clear and easy to scan

Design Approach

The visual direction focused on warmth, craftsmanship, and readability. I used a product-first layout, strong spacing, clean typography, and simple navigation patterns so users could move through the catalog naturally. Vendor descriptions and curated product presentation help the site feel more personal while still functioning like a practical online storefront.

Project Structure

The website includes multiple connected pages that support different parts of the shopping journey, including a homepage, an all-products catalog, a vendors page, vendor-specific product views, and individual product detail pages. This structure supports a more realistic marketplace experience and demonstrates how content can be scaled across different sections without losing consistency.

Outcome

The final result is a polished marketplace concept that presents local makers and handcrafted goods in a clear, engaging, and organized way. The project shows my ability to design and develop a multi-page website with category-based browsing, vendor-focused storytelling, and commerce-oriented interactions in a cohesive user experience.

Technologies

The project combines front-end design and development with a multi-page product browsing structure to create a functional shopping experience.
